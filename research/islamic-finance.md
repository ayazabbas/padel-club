## Islamic Finance Structures Explained

Understanding the key structures helps you evaluate options and have informed conversations with providers.

<details>
<summary><strong>Murabaha (Cost-Plus Financing)</strong></summary>

The most common structure for SME finance in the UK.

**How it works:**
1. The lender purchases the asset/commodity you need
2. They sell it to you at a pre-agreed markup (their profit)
3. You repay in fixed instalments

**Example:** You need Â£50,000 for court installation. The lender buys the equipment, sells it to you for Â£55,000 (Â£5,000 markup), payable over 24 months.

**Key points:**
- The markup is fixed upfront â€” no variable interest
- If you default, no additional late fees (in strict Sharia compliance)
- Used by: Qardus, SME Loans, QIB UK

**Source:** [Bank of England - What is Islamic Finance?](https://www.bankofengland.co.uk/explainers/what-is-islamic-finance)
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Musharakah (Partnership/Joint Venture)</strong></summary>

A profit-and-loss sharing arrangement â€” ideal for bringing in investors.

**How it works:**
1. Two or more parties contribute capital to a venture
2. Profits are shared according to a pre-agreed ratio
3. Losses are shared in proportion to capital contribution

**Example:** An investor contributes Â£50k to your padel club. You agree they receive 20% of profits until the investment is repaid, then 5% ongoing.

**Diminishing Musharakah:**
- Common for property â€” one party gradually buys out the other's share
- Used by Gatehouse Bank for home finance

**Best for:** Bringing in Muslim investors who want Sharia-compliant returns

**Source:** [Islamic Finance Guru](https://www.islamicfinanceguru.com/islamic-finance)
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Ijara (Lease)</strong></summary>

A Sharia-compliant leasing arrangement.

**How it works:**
1. The lessor (finance company) owns the asset
2. You pay rent to use it
3. At the end, the asset may transfer to you (Ijara wa Iqtina)

**Example:** A finance company buys padel court equipment and leases it to you for 5 years. At the end, ownership transfers.

**Key points:**
- The lessor bears ownership risk
- Maintenance responsibilities depend on the contract
- Similar to conventional operating lease

**Best for:** Equipment financing where you don't want upfront capital outlay

**Source:** [Hilton Smythe](https://hiltonsmythe.com/what-islamic-sharia-compliant-business-finance-is-available-in-the-uk/)
</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Qard Hasan (Interest-Free Loan)</strong></summary>

A benevolent loan with no profit for the lender.

**How it works:**
- The lender provides money as a loan
- The borrower repays only the principal â€” no markup
- Often used for charitable purposes

**Where to find:**
- Family and community (most common)
- Some Islamic charities
- Government grants function similarly (no repayment at all)

**Note:** Commercial providers rarely offer this as there's no business model.
</details>
